Afghan Mantu

Discover the exquisite flavors of Afghan Mantu, a traditional dumpling dish filled with a delightful mixture of ground meat and spices. Follow the steps below to create these bite-sized culinary delights.

In Afghan cuisine, the thinly rolled out dough of the mantu is filled with beef or lamb mixed with minced onions and spices, steamed and then topped with a yoghurt-based sauce. The sauce (seer mosst, lit. "garlic yoghurt") is made with chaka (thick, creamy, strained and salted yoghurt), lemon juice, dried and fresh mint, green and red chili powder and pressed garlic. The mantu can also be topped with a tomato-based sauce which can include split peas or red kidney beans and/or sautéed ground meat. This depends on the meat that was used for the filling of the mantu. The amount of yoghurt sauce is typically more than the tomato and ground meat sauce; the sauce is meant to be dotted on top so as to not cover the entire dish. However, separate dishes containing more of the ground meat, split peas, tomato sauce, and yoghurt sauce may also be kept at the table or dastarkhān. [23] Some Afghans also like to serve mantu with a carrot qorma or stew, instead of a tomato-based sauce. Now it is also famous in some areas of Pakistan due to Afghan refugees. [23] The authentic Afghan mantu dumplings are supposed to be small and bite-sized. The dough is supposed to be thinned out so that it is not chewy to bite on nor should one feel like they are eating more dough than filling. There is a specific pattern in which the dough of each dumpling is twisted and closed around the filling. There is a variation of this dish in Afghanistan known as aushak, in which the filling is different and it is made by boiling the dumplings instead of steaming them.

Total

1 hr + 30 min

Prep

1 hr

Cook

30 min

Ingredients

For 4 person

Quantity Ingredient
200g Chickpeas
250g Wheat Flour (Type 405)
1 packet Yeast
250ml Water
6 tsp Oil
1 tsp Salt
500g Onions
300g Mixed Ground Meat
As needed Salt
As needed Pepper
1 tsp Tomato Paste
1 tsp Sweet Paprika
1 tsp Ground Cumin
1 tbsp Dried Mint
1 clove Garlic
500g Yogurt
5 stems Coriander

Prepration

  1. Immerse the chickpeas in water and let them swell overnight for optimal texture and flavor. This extended soaking period promotes tenderness, eases digestion, and prepares them for the next stages of your culinary masterpiece.
  2. Combine flour, yeast, 150ml water, 2 teaspoons oil, and 0.5 teaspoons salt in a bowl and mix. The dough should be soft and not stick to your hands. Let the dough rest for about 30 minutes.
  3. Peel and finely dice 1 onion. Heat 2 teaspoons of oil in a pan, crumble and brown the minced meat. Add the diced onion and sauté briefly. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roll out the dough thinly and cut it into small squares. Place some of the minced meat filling in the center and fold the corners over the filling, sealing them together. Steam the Mantus in a steamer basket in a pot or on a sieve for about 30 minutes.
  5. Peel and dice the remaining onions. Heat 2 teaspoons of oil in a pot, sauté the onions, add tomato paste and spices. Cover everything with about 100ml of water and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. Then add the chickpeas and salt.
  6. Peel and finely chop the garlic. Mix it with yogurt and season with salt and pepper. Wash, dry, and chop the cilantro. Serve Mantus with stew and some yogurt, and sprinkle with cilantro.
